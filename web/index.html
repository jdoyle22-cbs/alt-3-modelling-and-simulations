ALT 3 - Report

By James Doyle

Investigation & Plan

The artefact is a model which simulates the number of tourists which will enter a country using the given inputs. I researched various sources across the internet for data relating to how various factors – mainly inflation, crime, disease and capacity – affect the amount of tourists which will travel to a country. These sources are listed in the footnote at the end. While I did attempt to find similar models, all that I found were how tourism affects the countries economy, or what defines tourism. The target users of this model are organisations like Fáilte Ireland, the Department of Enterprise, Tourism and Employment, or any Irish government body which may need to know how many tourists there may be in a given year. This may also be useful to non-governmental entities, e.g. people who run tourist attractions may benefit from extrapolating their local tourist numbers from this. [1] [2] [3]
